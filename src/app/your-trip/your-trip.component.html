WHERE TO EAT

The rúas around the Cathedral are full of restaurants and bars where you can enjoy delicious Galician tapas such as pulpo (octopus), empanada, tortilla... with a glass of Albariño or Ribeiro or a caña of Estrella Galicia, our local beer. Here are some recommendations:

O dezaséis: Traditional Galician tapas. Booking in advance is highly recommended.

La Tita: Their Spanish tortilla is very popular.

Abastos 2.0: Original and busy marketside eatery.

Other options:

  • A moa
  • A maceta

And if tapas are not your thing, here are a few restaurants we really like:

O curro da parra: Galician fine and creative cuisine.

O sendeiro: Galician food experience.

Café de Altamira: Seasonal Galician dishes with a contemporary twist.

Other options:

  • A Tafona
  • Restaurante Manso

WHERE TO DRINK

Anywhere. Santiago is full of tiny bars and terraces where you can enjoy some wine or beer. We recommend a wine route (or pub crawl) called “París-Dakar” along two of the rúas in the Old Town: Rúa do Franco and Rúa da Raíña. It has nothing to do with cars or raids though! There are two bars in Santiago called the París and the Dakar. You must begin your route at the París and drink a glass of wine in each of the bars on that street until you reach the last one, the Dakar. A COSTA DA MORTE

And if you decide to stay in Galicia for a few more days you can visit other Galician cities such as A Coruña, Pontevedra... or head towards the Death Coast! 

You can visit the Land's End (the Cape Finisterre), the Virxe da Barca Sanctuary in Muxía, enjoy a drink with a view in Lires, walk along Carnota's beach or find the hidden waterfall in Ézaro.
