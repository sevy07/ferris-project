YOUR TRIP TO SANTIAGO

Santiago de Compostela is the capital of the Spanish region of Galicia. It is known as the final stop on the epic Camino de Santiago pilgrimage trail. Its old town is a World Heritage Site, and its arcaded stone streets are impregnated with art, history and legends. 

You cannot leave Santiago without visiting the Cathedral, which is the reputed burial place of St James the Great, a grand Romanesque structure with later Gothic and Baroque additions, as well as the prazas (squares) and narrow rúas (streets) surrounding it. 

GETTING THERE

Santiago de Compostela has an airport with direct flights to and from London (with Ryanair, Easyjet and Vueling), Madrid, Barcelona, Paris (Vueling) and other European destinations. 

A Coruña, a city which is only half an hour away from Santiago and that most of you probably know because of its wonderful football team (go Dépor go!), also has an airport with direct flights to and from London (with Vueling).

Sometimes flying out to Porto in Northern Portugal can be a good option too. There are many flights to and from London, Paris, Lyon, Toulouse, among other destinations. Then it's possible to travel to Santiago by coach from Porto's airport. The journey takes about three hours. 

And of course, for the bravest among you there is one more option: el Camino! But start getting your pilgrim boots ready, as it will take you a few months to reach Santiago and we wouldn't want you to miss such a special occasion! :p

WHERE TO DRINK

Anywhere. Santiago is full of tiny bars and terraces where you can enjoy some wine or beer. We recommend a wine route (or pub crawl) called “París-Dakar” along two of the rúas in the Old Town: Rúa do Franco and Rúa da Raíña. It has nothing to do with cars or raids though! There are two bars in Santiago called the París and the Dakar. You must begin your route at the París and drink a glass of wine in each of the bars on that street until you reach the last one, the Dakar.
